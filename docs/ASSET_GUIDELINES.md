# Asset Guidelines

**Status:** Canonical. Permanent project documentation — not sprint notes.
**Scope:** Production guidelines for anyone creating a Product Asset or a
World Asset — the illustration style every asset must be produced in, and
the styles it must never slip into. This document does not create any
artwork. It relates to `docs/STUDIO_DESIGN_CANON.md` as follows: the
Design Canon defines VihuStudio's product-wide visual and emotional
identity (palette, spacing, interaction, motion); this document is the
narrower, production-facing counterpart an artist or asset generator
follows when producing a specific file. The two must never contradict
each other — if they ever appear to, the Design Canon wins, and this
document should be corrected to match it.

---

## Illustration Style

Every Product Asset and World Asset — illustrations, environments, UI
textures, World hero art — is produced as:

- **Hand painted.** It should look like it was painted by a person, not
  generated by a flat vector tool.
- **Watercolour.** Soft edges, translucent layered colour, gentle colour
  bleed — not hard-edged flat fill.
- **Children's storybook.** The reference point is a picture book
  illustration, not a marketing illustration or a stock graphic.
- **Visible paper texture.** A faint grain should read through the
  image, the way real watercolour paper does — never a perfectly smooth
  digital gradient.
- **Soft brush strokes.** Edges and details carry visible, gentle brush
  texture rather than crisp vector precision.
- **Warm colour palette.** Cream, sage, warm sand, soft sky, lavender —
  the same family documented in `docs/STUDIO_DESIGN_CANON.md` §3. Gold
  is reserved as the one accent colour, never a base illustration tone.

## Never

- **Corporate UI.** Nothing that reads as a business tool's chrome.
- **Enterprise styling.** No dense information density, no admin-panel
  visual language.
- **Dashboard icons.** No generic flat-outline icon-set style for
  anything meant to carry emotional weight (Product Illustrations,
  environments, World hero art). Small functional icons
  (`assets/icons/`) are the one place a simpler glyph is appropriate,
  and even those should feel warm and rounded, not clinical.
- **Flat SaaS design.** No flat-vector-with-drop-shadow style common to
  productivity software marketing pages.

If a produced asset reminds a reviewer of any of the products named in
`docs/STUDIO_DESIGN_CANON.md` §9 ("Things VihuStudio Should Never Look
Like"), it fails this guideline regardless of how polished it is.

---

## Where this applies

These guidelines govern both halves of the Product/World asset split
(`docs/PRODUCT_ASSETS.md`):

- **Product Assets** — the Product Illustration family
  (`docs/PRODUCT_ILLUSTRATIONS.md`), the environments
  (`docs/ENVIRONMENTS.md`), and all shared UI art under `assets/ui/`.
- **World Assets** — every asset a World packages inside its own
  `.vtheme` (`docs/WORLD_ASSET_CONTRACT.md`). A World's hero art must
  feel like it belongs in the same universe as the Product Illustrations
  a child saw on Screen 1 — one consistent illustration language across
  both halves of the split, even though the two are never stored
  together.
